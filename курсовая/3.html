Top 8 most profitable IT companies in the world in 2021

1. Dell Technologies$92 billion)

Industry: Computer software, cloud computing. Market value: $55 billion Employees: 165,000 people. Today Dell is considered the most profitable IT company in the world. Its annual profit is $92 billion, and the number of customers worldwide exceeds the 10 million mark. Interestingly, almost 99% of Fortune 500 companies cooperate with this "IT" giant. Dell was founded in 1984 by 19-year-old student Michael Dell. Then his main idea was to sell PCs with adaptive configurations — when buying, the client could choose any necessary components that would fully meet his individual needs. Over time, the company has become one of the most popular manufacturers of PCs and equipment. But recently, thanks to its subsidiaries EMC, Pivotal, RSA, Secureworks, Virtustream, and VMware, it has begun to focus more on software, cloud storage, virtual reality, IT infrastructures, data centers and digital security. In 2015, Dell acquired EMC for $67 billion, which is still considered one of the most expensive purchases in the IT industry. The company operates in 180 countries around the world. The headquarters is located in Round Rock, Texas, USA.

2. IBM $77 billion

Industry: Cloud computing, AI, computer software. Market value: $115 billion. Employees: 384,000 people. IBM is the second most profitable IT company in the world with annual revenue exceeding $77 billion. And this is not strange, considering that almost 97% of the world's banks and 80% of retail companies use the products and services of this company. The company International Business Machines (IBM) or "Blue Giant" (Big Blue), as it is sometimes called, was founded in 1911 by Charles Flint. During its long history, IBM has given our world many great inventions, including floppy disks, hard drives, magnetic stripe on credit cards, barcode system and much more. By the way, the first ATM also appeared thanks to the company's mainframe. Today, some of IBM's most popular services are related to cloud and cognitive computing, IT infrastructure, computer security, the Internet of Things, data analysis, blockchain and artificial intelligence (AI). Throughout its existence, the company has had more than 130 acquisitions and mergers. But the most expensive deal is considered to be with Red Hat worth $34 billion. IBM has offices in more than 170 countries around the world. The main office is located in Armonk, New York, USA.

3. Cisco Systems $49 billion

Industry: Network software. Market value: $192 billion. Employees: 77,500 people. Cisco Systems is the third most successful IT company in the world by revenue. Its annual profit has already exceeded $50 billion. Cisco is usually known as a manufacturer of switches, routers and other network equipment. But since 2015, the company has decided to delve into innovative areas related to software. Today Cisco is also engaged in the Internet of Things, cybersecurity, cloud computing, data centers and data analysis. The company was founded in 1984 by Leonard Bosak and Sandra Lerner. They created a commercially successful multiprotocol router that connected previously incompatible computers and was built on the basis of the Stanford Blue Box Router. Interestingly, in 2000 Cisco was the most expensive company in the world, thanks to the so-called Dot-com bubble. The company's market capitalization has reached a record high of $500 billion. Since 1993, Cisco has absorbed approximately 170 different enterprises. Today, it has offices in more than 160 countries, and the main office is located in San Jose, California, USA.

4. Accenture $44 billion

Industry: Business consulting, strategy, technology, processes. Market value: $169 billion Employees: 506,000 people. Accenture was created as a technology division of an accounting firm called "Arthur Andersen", but in 1989 it turned into an independent company. Today it is one of the largest companies operating in the field of digital marketing and providing business consulting, technological solutions, security and productivity applications, etc. But the company is not going to stop there. The next priority and rather ambitious step for Accenture is quantum computing. Today, the company serves customers from more than 120 countries. Accenture's customer base even includes such giants as Microsoft, Oracle and SAP. And yes, if you don't know, the name "Accenture" comes from the phrase "accent on the future" (the accent of the future). The company's head office is located in Dublin, Ireland.

5. Oracle $39 billion

Industry: Enterprise software, cloud programming. Market value: $181 billion Employees: 135,000 people. Oracle is known for its ERP systems, user-friendly applications and effective solutions, which have already attracted more than 400 thousand customers from 175 countries. The company was founded by Larry Ellison, Robert Miner and Edward Oates in 1977. Since then, she has been developing database-based marketing applications and technologies, cloud computing, database management systems, enterprise applications, etc. Today Oracle is also interested in "smart" systems, integration of cloud platforms, tools for analysis based on machine learning, AI, the Internet of things, blockchain and many other interesting things. Oracle is headquartered in Redwood City, California, USA.

6. Fujitsu $37 billion

Industry: Software, IT services and consulting, telecommunications. Market value: $31 billion Employees: 129,000 people. Fujitsu is one of the largest IT service providers in Japan and one of the leading software companies in the world. It was founded in 1935 as a manufacturing subsidiary of Fuji Electric Limited. But since then, the company has made its way into the world of information technology and software. This can be especially seen if we analyze the history of mergers: the company sold its production units more often, and acquired mainly companies that deal with software and cloud computing. Today, Fujitsu provides personal and corporate programming, information storage, and system integration services. In addition, the company has a department that deals with IT consulting, implementation and management. The company operates in more than 70 countries and has clients in more than 100 countries around the world. Fujitsu can also boast of many interesting partnerships with such giants as Microsoft or Siemens. The main office is located in Tokyo, Japan.

7. Tech Data $37 billion

Industry: Technology, Software, IT consulting. Market value: $5 billion. Employees: 15,000 people. Tech Data does not belong to software developers, but is a supplier of IT products. The company was founded in 1974 thanks to Edward Reimund. Basically, she was engaged in the supply of computer supplies to Florida hospitals and government agencies. But in 1993, Tech Data turned into a full-fledged distributor of PC products. For the first time, the company added software to the list of products offered in 1992. Then more than 40 companies offering various software solutions and applications became its partners. Microsoft was among the first. Today Tech Data is one of the richest and most reliable IT companies in the world. Its customer base includes more than 22 thousand enterprises, including Google, Cisco, Dell, HPE, HPI, LG, Samsung, LG, Lenovo, IBM and many other famous names. The company is headquartered in Clearwater, Florida, USA.

8. SAP $31 billion

Industry: Corporate software. Market value: $151 billion. Employees: 100,330 people. SAP is a fairly strong developer of business applications, which is engaged in the creation of SaaS and enterprise software, as well as consulting. Dietmar Hopp and Hasso Plattner founded the company in 1972, and the following year production of its first commercial product, the financial accounting system, began. Today, enterprise applications and developments cover many business areas, such as finance, personnel, production, marketing, engineering, etc. By the way, it was SAP that created the first ERP system for supply and logistics management. Over the past few years, the company has also been engaged in machine learning, the Internet of Things, blockchain and cloud computing. SAP has many offices and representative offices in more than 130 countries around the world. The headquarters is located in Waldorf, Germany.
